The shift

Surveillance isn't new. A CCTV camera in 1995 recorded a street. Today, your phone records you, your contacts, your location, your heart rate, who you texted at 3 a.m. — and sends it to a company you've never met. The question isn't whether you're watched. It's who benefits, and whether you can ever see them back.

Why is that important?

Language

profiling
Building a detailed picture of who you are. For profit.
tracking
Following you continuously. Where. When. Why.
harvesting
Collecting data. Not asking. Just taking.
asymmetry
Imbalance of power. You watch Netflix. Netflix watches you.
consent
Real agreement. Clicking "I agree" without reading isn't consent.
chilling effect
Being watched stops you from speaking. Fear changes behaviour.
