The shift

Read this carefully.

Surveillance isn't new. What's new is the asymmetry. A CCTV camera in 1995 recorded a street. A smartphone in 2026 records you, your contacts, your location, your heart rate, and who you texted at 3 a.m. — and sends it to a company you've never met, under a contract you didn't read. The question isn't whether you're being watched. It's who benefits from watching you, and whether you can ever see them back.

Language

Words that matter now

profiling
Building a detailed picture of who you are based on your data. Not random — targeted.
tracking
Following someone continuously. Where they go, what they do, when they do it.
data harvesting
Collecting your information systematically. Not asking. Just taking.
asymmetry
Imbalance. One side has power; the other doesn't. You watch Netflix. Netflix watches you.
consent
Agreement. Real consent means you understand what you're agreeing to. Clicking "I agree" isn't consent.
a chilling effect
When you know you're being watched, you stop speaking freely. Fear shapes behaviour.
